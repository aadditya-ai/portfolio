Understanding 10DLC: A Complete Guide for Businesses

Published: Jan 15, 2026 • 5 min read • Trust & Safety

If you're running SMS campaigns in the United States, understanding 10DLC (10-Digit Long Code) is critical for maintaining deliverability and compliance in 2026. This guide covers everything you need to know about 10DLC registration and best practices.

What is 10DLC?

10DLC stands for "10-Digit Long Code" — it's a phone number type used for Application-to-Person (A2P) messaging. Unlike short codes (typically 5-6 digits), 10DLC numbers look like regular phone numbers but are specifically registered and vetted for business messaging.

The 10DLC system was introduced by major US carriers (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon) to combat SMS spam and improve message deliverability. It requires businesses to register their company information, use cases, and messaging campaigns before sending commercial messages.

Why 10DLC Matters

Before 10DLC, businesses could send marketing SMS from standard long codes without much oversight. This led to significant spam problems. Carriers responded with aggressive filtering, which hurt legitimate businesses.

10DLC solves this by creating a trust framework:

  • → Better deliverability: Registered campaigns see higher throughput and fewer blocks
  • → Compliance assurance: Built-in vetting ensures you meet carrier requirements
  • → Higher volume: Properly registered campaigns can send significantly more messages per day
  • → Reputation protection: Separates legitimate business messaging from spam

The Registration Process

Step 1: Brand Registration

First, register your business (your "brand"). You'll need:

  • Legal business name and address
  • Tax ID (EIN for US businesses)
  • Business website
  • Business type and industry
  • Contact information

Your brand receives a "trust score" from The Campaign Registry (TCR), which affects your messaging capabilities. Higher scores = higher throughput and better treatment.

Step 2: Campaign Registration

After brand registration, you register each messaging campaign. This includes:

  • Use case: Marketing, 2FA, customer care, mixed, etc.
  • Message flow: Example messages and opt-in/opt-out language
  • Volume estimates: How many messages you'll send
  • Privacy policy and terms: Links to your compliance documentation

Step 3: Number Association

Once your campaign is approved, you associate specific 10DLC phone numbers with that campaign. These numbers can now send messages under the campaign's approved use case.

Best Practices for 10DLC

✓ Do This

  • → Get explicit opt-in consent before sending
  • → Provide clear opt-out instructions in every message
  • → Match your campaign use case to actual message content
  • → Monitor bounce rates and complaints closely
  • → Keep your brand information up-to-date

✗ Avoid This

  • → Sending without proper registration
  • → Using deceptive messaging or fake sender names
  • → Ignoring opt-out requests
  • → Purchasing phone lists without consent
  • → Sharing numbers across multiple unrelated campaigns

Common Challenges and Solutions

Challenge: Low trust score limiting throughput
Solution: Verify your business through external vetting services, maintain a professional website, and ensure your business information is accurate and consistent.

Challenge: Campaign rejections
Solution: Provide clear, detailed use case descriptions. Include sample messages that accurately represent what you'll send. Be transparent about your opt-in process.

Challenge: Managing multiple campaigns
Solution: Use a Campaign Management Service (CMS) or work with a CPaaS provider (like Twilio, Sinch) that handles registration complexities.

Looking Ahead

10DLC continues to evolve. Carriers are tightening requirements and improving filtering algorithms. The key to success is treating 10DLC as an ongoing compliance program, not a one-time registration.

Stay informed about carrier updates, monitor your messaging metrics, and maintain clean sending practices. The effort you put into proper 10DLC implementation directly translates to better deliverability and customer reach.
